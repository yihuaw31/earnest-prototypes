# Earnest Prototypes

## Retrospective Instructions

Whenever Eva asks you to summarize, reflect, or do a retrospective on the session,
always include a "What felt rough" section that honestly reviews the prototyping
process — not just what was built. See the retrospective format below.

### The prompt

Look back at everything we built and discussed in this session and give me an honest retrospective focused specifically on friction and failure points. I don't need a recap of what we made — I need to understand what wasn't working so I can prompt better next time.

Structure your response as follows:

**What required the most back-and-forth**
Which prompts or instructions needed multiple rounds of clarification before you got it right? What was unclear or underspecified in my original request?

**What came out wrong on the first try**
Which visual outputs, layouts, or interactions missed the mark initially? What did you misinterpret, and why — was it vague direction, conflicting instructions, or a technical limitation?

**Where I had to over-specify**
Which things should have worked from a brief description but required me to go into unnecessary detail to get right? These are places where either my prompting style or your defaults need adjusting.

**Patterns I should know about**
Based on this session, what are 2–3 specific things I should do differently when prompting you to build prototypes? Be direct — this is for me to improve my process.

**What Claude Code genuinely can't do well (yet)**
Be honest about any technical or design limitations that got in the way — things where the output was poor not because of the prompt but because of real constraints. I'd rather know than keep trying to work around something that won't improve.
