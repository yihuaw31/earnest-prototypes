The problem

You're not confused about money.
You're confused about which life to choose.

You're saving, earning well, maybe ahead of schedule. But the next questions don't have obvious answers.

"Can we move abroad and still keep our home here?"

Rental income, foreign taxes, cost of living gaps. You want the honest math before you fall in love with the idea.

"What does having a baby really do to our finances over 10 years?"

Not just the stroller. Childcare, lost income, home size, career pauses. You want the honest number.

"How much more do we actually need to retire early?"

Your situation is messier than a Reddit rule of thumb. You want a real answer based on your numbers.

These aren't questions about discipline. They're about illegibility — you can't see how the decision unfolds, so you stay frozen. Paths makes it visible.
How it works

Three steps to clarity.

1

10-year projections with plain-language summaries. See the tradeoffs clearly enough to commit.
